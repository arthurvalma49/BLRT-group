# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

B2B buyers and technical decision-makers in the maritime industry: fleet operators, ship owners, port agents, and procurement managers coordinating inspection schedules. They evaluate vendors under time pressure, often from a port or vessel operations context. They are experienced, skeptical of marketing language, and respond to credentials, certifications, and operational specifics.

## Product Purpose

Tehnomet Survey OÜ (BLRT Grupp) is an independent marine vessel inspection company based in Tallinn, Estonia. The site exists to establish technical authority, communicate service scope and class-society approval, and generate qualified inquiries. Success is a prospect who understands exactly what Tehnomet does, trusts their credentials, and submits an inspection request.

## Brand Personality

Reliable · Discreet · Industrial

Quiet competence. No marketing rhetoric. The tone is that of an expert who doesn't need to persuade — they state facts and let their record speak. Emotions to evoke: confidence, precision, trust.

Reference aesthetic: Palantir.com / Primer.ai — technical intelligence, dark surfaces, data-forward, no-nonsense. Not startup energy, not consulting warmth. Serious operational capability.

## Anti-references

- Bloated enterprise SaaS (Salesforce, SAP): committee-designed, blue-on-blue corporate mediocrity, padded with marketing copy that says nothing
- Outdated port-authority government sites: tables, dated serif-heavy layouts, 1990s web structure
- Generic maritime stock-photo aesthetic: anchor imagery, wave patterns, "setting sail for success" headlines

## Design Principles

1. **Credentials over claims.** Let certifications, class societies, and measurable facts carry authority. No superlatives, no empty "world-class" language.
2. **Precision in everything.** Spacing, copy, and data should feel measured and exact — the same discipline the company applies to ultrasonic measurement.
3. **Discreet confidence.** The design should feel like a firm handshake, not a sales pitch. Restraint signals expertise.
4. **Industrial materiality.** The visual language should echo the physical world the company operates in: steel, precision instruments, technical documentation. Not softened or consumer-facing.
5. **Global reach, specific knowledge.** Communicate worldwide operational capability through concrete specifics (ports, class societies, response times), never vague geographic claims.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Existing multilingual support: EN, RU, ET. No additional certification requirements specified. Reduced-motion preference should be respected for any animation.
