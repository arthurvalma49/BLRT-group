# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Ship managers, vessel owners, and maritime procurement and safety officers evaluating BLRT Grupp companies for repair, inspection, engineering, materials, or port services. They operate in a high-stakes, time-pressured environment: budgets are significant, downtime is expensive, and vendor credibility must be verifiable. They are operationally focused, experienced, and skeptical of marketing language. They respond to certifications, class-society approvals, concrete response times, and named ports. They do not respond to superlatives.

## Product Purpose

The BLRT Grupp site establishes the group's industrial authority across six sectors and routes qualified inquiries to the right company. The group operates 17 companies in 5 countries across ship repair, shipbuilding, inspection and testing, engineering, steel and materials, and port services. Success is a prospect who immediately understands which BLRT company handles their need, trusts their credentials and capacity, and initiates contact.

## Brand Personality

Reliable · Raw · Industrial

The look and feel of heavy engineering done right: machined steel, drydock scale, classification rigour. Quiet competence. No marketing rhetoric. The tone is that of an expert who doesn't need to persuade: they state facts and let their record speak. Emotions to evoke: confidence, precision, trust.

Reference aesthetic: Rolls-Royce Marine / DNV / Maersk — authoritative physical-world capability, not digital-product polish. The interface should feel like a technical document prepared by people who have been at sea, not a SaaS product designed by a growth team.

## Anti-references

- Bloated enterprise SaaS (Salesforce, SAP): committee-designed blue-on-blue corporate mediocrity padded with marketing copy that says nothing
- Outdated port-authority government sites: tables, dated serif-heavy layouts, 1990s web structure
- Generic maritime stock-photo aesthetic: anchor imagery, wave patterns, "setting sail for success" headlines
- Dark "hacker" aesthetic: neon on black, code terminals, developer-tool visual language
- Startup landing page patterns: hero CTAs, feature grids, social proof carousels

## Design Principles

1. **Credentials over claims.** Class societies, certifications, measurable facts, and named ports carry authority. No superlatives, no empty "world-class" language. If a claim can't be sourced, cut it.
2. **Industrial materiality.** Visual language echoes the physical world the company operates in: steel plate, precision instruments, technical documentation. Not softened, not consumer-facing.
3. **Scale communicates capability.** The group's breadth (17 companies, 6 sectors, 5 countries) should feel like mass and weight, not a feature list. Spatial composition and typographic scale do this work.
4. **Discreet confidence.** Restraint signals expertise. The design should feel like a firm handshake, not a sales pitch. Every decorative element must earn its place against an industrial standard.
5. **Contact is the conversion.** Every page routes toward a qualified inquiry. No friction between "I need this service" and "here is who to call."

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Existing multilingual support: EN, RU, ET. Reduced-motion preference respected for all animation. No additional certification requirements specified.
